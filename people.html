Who We Are

Co-founder & Director
Holly Kaufman
Climate change, plastics & sustainability expert · LinkedIn

Holly is a Senior Fellow at World Resources Institute (WRI), serving as a thought leader at the nexus of climate change and plastics. She is a Nominator for Prince William's Earthshot Prize, focused on climate and plastics, and a member of the California Senate Bill 54 Implementation Working Group — a stakeholder group advising on the nation's first state plastic packaging reduction law.

Holly is founder and President of Environment & Enterprise Strategies, providing strategic guidance to the Union of Concerned Scientists, NSF, the White House Council on Environmental Quality, the Elders, Pew Charitable Trusts, C40, the World Economic Forum, NRDC, and STAND.earth, among others. She managed the climate change & national security portfolio at the U.S. Department of Defense as a President Clinton appointee, and was a strategy and outreach expert on the UN climate treaty negotiations team at the State Department, where she received a Superior Team Honor Award.

Holly holds a B.S. with Highest Honors from UC Berkeley and an MPA in international environmental and economic studies from the Harvard Kennedy School.

Co-founder & Research Director Emeritus
Dr. Alice (Xia) Zhu
Environmental analytical scientist — plastics lifecycle, climate change & oceanography · Website · LinkedIn · Publications

Alice co-founded The Plastics & Climate Project and led the scientific research as lead author of the Project's peer-reviewed paper published in Frontiers in Environmental Science. She now serves on the Project's Advisory Board.

Alice is a Banting Postdoctoral Fellow at Memorial University in Newfoundland studying the fate of microplastics in the Atlantic Ocean, and a member of the UNEP/European Commission's "Project on Plastic Pollution Toolkit" Working Group. She is the recipient of the Vanier Canada Research Scholarship and numerous other awards including the Queen Elizabeth II Graduate Scholarship in Science & Technology.

She received her PhD from the University of Toronto (Rochman lab) and was a Visiting Research Scholar at Scripps Institution of Oceanography and at CSIRO.

Communications Strategist
Pamela Wellner
Communications & Campaign Strategist for Climate & Sustainability · LinkedIn

Pamela Wellner is Chief Strategist and founder of Amplify Eco, a communications and program management consulting practice dedicated to advancing climate, sustainability, and human rights initiatives. With over 30 years of experience, she has led integrated communications and campaign strategies for mission-driven organizations including Christensen Global, Solar AquaGrid, Health Care Without Harm, ClimateWorks, Greenpeace, and the Rainforest Action Network.

She is board chair of Charge Across Town, which increases equitable access to clean transportation. She holds an MPA from New York University and a BA in Human Ecology from the College of the Atlantic.

For media inquiries: pwellner@amplifyeco.com

Advisory Board

Scientific & Policy Advisors

The Plastics & Climate Project benefits from the input of advisory board members who ensure the scientific integrity of the Project's work and provide strategic advice. The Advisory Board also enables people and institutions engaged in complementary issues to add value to each other's work.

Research professor of oceanography, Sea Education Association. Research areas include ocean circulation, distribution of marine plastic debris, and degradation and fate of plastics in the ocean.

Key architect of the UN global plastics treaty research and proposal, Australian National Centre for Ocean Resources & Security, University of Wollongong. Co-author of Climate Impact of Plastics (GRID-Arendal, 2024) and contributing author to the Project's report.

SR

Oceanographer, research scientist at the Center for Marine Debris Research at Hawaii Pacific University. One of the first scientists to discover that plastic emits greenhouse gases when exposed to sunlight.

Professor of environmental politics at UC Santa Barbara. Research includes climate change, renewable energy, water, and chemicals policy. Named on TIME100 Next and Business Insider's top 30 global leaders working toward climate solutions.
