Summary Report

Plastics: Exposing Their Climate Impacts

"We now know that plastics, from their start to their waste, are polluting the oceans and also fueling the climate crisis. This new research makes it clear: every stage of the plastic lifecycle emits greenhouse gases, and plastics may undermine the ocean's ability to store carbon. The evidence is growing, but so are the gaps in our understanding, and we have to fill in the gaps in our knowledge."

— Dr. Sylvia Earle

Plastics: Exposing Their Climate Impacts

Holly Kaufman & Dr. Xia (Alice) Zhu, The Plastics and Climate Project · Cecilia Diedrich & Dr. John Doherty, Environmental Law Institute · With key input from the Monterey Bay Aquarium & Dr. Karen Raubenheimer, University of Wollongong, Australia

This is the first report to comprehensively assess the existing peer-reviewed data on the climate impacts of plastics. It analyzes existing peer-reviewed data, identifies critical data gaps, lays out a roadmap for continued research, and recommends policies and actions to support that research and to include plastics' climate impacts in relevant models, scenarios, accounting, and analyses.

This report is a non-technical summary of the Project's peer-reviewed paper, "The knowns and unknowns in our understanding of how plastics impact climate change: A systematic review," published in April 2025 in Frontiers in Environmental Science.

The report offers recommendations for public and private sector actions to spur the needed research and to ensure that the climate impacts of plastics are considered in relevant assessments and policies — intended to raise awareness and motivate action by policymakers, scientists, industry, investors, educators, and non-governmental and community representatives.

Peer-Reviewed Paper

Frontiers in Environmental Science

"The knowns and unknowns in our understanding of how plastics impact climate change: A systematic review"

Published April 2025 · Frontiers in Environmental Science

These publications outline the current understanding of how plastics affect the climate across their lifecycle — through the emission of greenhouse gases, impacts on the carbon cycle, and through radiative effects. The authors highlight significant data gaps in quantifying these impacts and propose a research agenda to address these shortcomings.

The authors emphasize the need for more comprehensive studies on greenhouse gas emissions at every stage of the plastic lifecycle, the effects of plastic pollution on carbon sinks in various ecosystems, and the influence of plastic particles on Earth's energy balance.
